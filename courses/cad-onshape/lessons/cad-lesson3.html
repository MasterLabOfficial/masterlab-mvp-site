CAD Lesson 3 – Sketch Strategy & Construction Geometry

Good models start with clean sketches. This lesson focuses on how to plan sketches, use construction geometry, and avoid messy, overcomplicated profiles.

  • Start with the simplest shape that represents the main form.
  • Add detail with additional features, not one giant sketch.
  • Use centerlines to control symmetry and dimensions.

Construction lines (often dashed) are used for references, not solid edges.

  • Use construction lines for centers, axes, and symmetry.
  • Dimension to construction geometry to keep sketches clean.
  • Onshape shortcut: toggle construction with Q.
  • Too many entities in one sketch.
  • Using edges of features instead of reference geometry.
  • Leaving sketches underdefined (blue) when they matter.
