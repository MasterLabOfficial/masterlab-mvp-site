CAD Lesson 6 – Assemblies & Mates in Onshape

Assemblies show how parts fit and move together. In Onshape, you use mates to define relationships between parts.

  • Part Studio: Where you create parts.
  • Assembly: Where you bring parts together and define motion.
  • Fastened: No movement between parts.
  • Revolute: Rotational motion around one axis.
  • Slider: Linear motion along one axis.
  • Cylindrical: Rotation + sliding along an axis.
  • Planar: Sliding in a plane.

Onshape uses mate connectors as reference points for mates. Choosing good mate connector locations makes assemblies easier to control.
