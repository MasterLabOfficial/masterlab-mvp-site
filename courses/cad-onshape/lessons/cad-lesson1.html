CAD Lesson 1 – Onshape & Sketching Fundamentals

In this lesson, you’ll learn the core ideas behind CAD in Onshape: what sketches are, how constraints work, and how to fully define geometry.

CAD (Computer‑Aided Design) is used to create precise 2D sketches and 3D models. In Onshape, everything starts with a sketch, which you then turn into 3D features like extrudes, revolves, and cuts.

  • Documents: Cloud‑based containers for your work.
  • Part Studios: Where you create parts and sketches.
  • Assemblies: Where you mate parts together.
  • Features: Extrude, Revolve, Fillet, Chamfer, Shell, Pattern, etc.

Constraints control how geometry behaves. Common ones in Onshape:

  • Horizontal / Vertical: Force lines to be horizontal or vertical.
  • Coincident: A point lies on another point or edge.
  • Concentric: Circles share the same center.
  • Parallel / Perpendicular: Lines never intersect or meet at 90°.
  • Tangent: A curve touches a line or circle at exactly one point.
  • Equal: Makes lengths or radii identical.
  • Symmetric: Geometry mirrors across a line.
  • Fix: Locks geometry in place.

In Onshape, a sketch is:

  • Black: Fully defined (good).
  • Blue: Underdefined (missing constraints or dimensions).
  • Red: Overdefined (conflicting constraints or dimensions).

Your goal is to make important sketches fully defined using a mix of constraints and dimensions.

Use the Dimension (D) tool to set:

  • Line lengths
  • Circle diameters
  • Distances between points
  • Angles between lines
