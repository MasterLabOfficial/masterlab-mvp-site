CAD Lesson 10 – Common Mistakes & Competition Strategy

This lesson focuses on avoiding common CAD mistakes and thinking strategically during timed events like Science Olympiad.

Common Modeling Mistakes

  • Underdefined sketches (blue) on critical features.
  • Over‑reliance on edges instead of reference geometry.
  • One giant sketch instead of multiple simple ones.

Common Onshape Pitfalls

  • Forgetting to select the correct Part Studio or Assembly tab.
  • Misusing mates, causing over‑constrained assemblies.
  • Not naming features or parts, making models hard to read.

Competition Strategy

  • Model simple but clean—don't chase unnecessary detail.
  • Use patterns, symmetry, and variables to move faster.
  • Know your shortcuts: D, L, C, R, T, N, F, Q.
